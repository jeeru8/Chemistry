Did you know that all matter is particulate in nature? Scientifically, this means that between separate bits of matter there are spaces, which contain no matter.

Every pure substance can be distinguished from one another by their physical and chemical properties.

Physical properties can be observed or measured without changing the fixed composition of a substance. These include color, odor, hardness, melting, and boiling points.

Chemical properties are those that describe how substance interacts or fail to interact with other substances to form new ones. For example, iron reacts with moist air to form rust.

Can you give examples of objects with their physical and chemical properties?

Chapter 1

Extensive properties depend on how much of a particular sample is on hand. Volume, mass, height, and length are some of the examples of this property.


Intensive properties are those that do not depend on the size of the sample. For example, ice melts at 00C regardless of the size of an ice. Examples of intensive properties are density, boiling, and freezing points. Can you think of other extensive and intensive properties of matter?
